Leadership · Northeastern University

Student Facilitator – GLI

Graduate Leadership Institute · Boston, MA · May 2025

It started with a casual Instagram scroll. I saw a post about the Graduate Leadership Institute at Northeastern, thought "why not?" and applied on a whim. I didn't make the first cut, landed on the waitlist, and then a day later got the confirmation I needed. That small moment of persistence led to one of the most transformative experiences of my graduate journey.

The program was a 6-week deep dive into 12 leadership competencies, two per week, covering everything from adaptability and communication to global awareness and empowerment. From the very first session, I knew this was different. The opening icebreaker set the tone perfectly: instead of introducing yourself, you had 5 minutes to learn everything about the person beside you, their name, their program, their story, and then introduce them to the room. It was humbling, connective, and oddly exhilarating. I found myself counting down to each Wednesday session, something I rarely do for any class. Every week brought new challenges, team exercises, and honest conversations that tested not just our leadership knowledge but our patience, creativity, and self-awareness. The biggest lesson? A true leader doesn't stand above their team. They make every person feel equally seen, heard, and valued. We closed with a graduation ceremony that I'll genuinely never forget, full of laughter, reflection, and friendships built across six intense, beautiful weeks.

A term later, I came back, this time as a Student Facilitator for the Summer 2025 cohort. Alongside two fellow facilitators from previous cohorts, I now held space for 30 new graduate peers, the same way my facilitators once held it for me. Seeing the program through both lenses, as a student and as a guide, gave me a depth of perspective I didn't expect. I carried the knowledge forward, but more importantly, I carried the belief that leadership isn't a title. It's a practice.
